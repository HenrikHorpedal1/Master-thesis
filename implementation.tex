\section{Motors and motor brackets}

"If using 3D printed mounts, care should be taken to ensure the mount does not warp. PLA and other low temperature filaments will warp under load at room temperature, and will do so even more when exposed to thermal heating like from a motor controller. It is recommended to use PETG, PC, or a filament designed to not warp at the operating temperature you intend."
\citep{mjbots_moteus_mechanical_setup}

The brackets for the Moteus boards were 3D-printed in PETG in two parts and assembled to the motors using M2.5 screws,

\section{Electronics}
\subsection{Power}
Power cables for the Moteus motor divers were constructed by soldering a XT30 connector on one end, following the instructions from Moteus
(link).

A fuse holder was joined to the 16 AWG wire with a butt splice and the other end of the cable was termintated with
bootlace ferrules for secure connection to the terminals of the Power supply unit. The cable is shown in \autoref{fig:powercable}.

To facilitate for testing the system more safely a box was made to house the powersupply unit, to not have exposed mains voltage on the terminals of the PSU.

\subsection{Drum}

The pieces to make up the drum was lasercut out of 6mm mdf,
\begin{figure}[H]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.4\textwidth]{fig/implementation/lasercut-drum-pieces.png}\hfill
	\includegraphics[width=0.4\textwidth]{fig/implementation/drum-stacked-pieces.png}\hfill
	\caption{}
	\label{fig:lasercut-drum-pieces}
\end{figure}

The scoop, the curved surface in (referer til design) was made by two sheets of bent plywood. this was done the following way:
The two pieces of plywood were lasercut. Then a jig for bending was designed and implemented (se appendix). Then the two plywood pieces were soaked in warm water for 30 minutes, and then clamped in the jig for 24 hours, then the pieces were glued together and clamped in the jig for another 24 hours.
